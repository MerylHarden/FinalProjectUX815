STEWARD

A RISING TIDE LIFTS ALL BOATS

As a Black American designer, my work is informed by lived experience and cultural historical knowledge. The Steward logo draws from Adinkra symbols, a visual language from West Africa, specifically regions now known as Ghana and Côte d’Ivoire. These symbols carry meaning across generations, communicating wisdom, values, beliefs, and ways of understanding the world. I chose the Aprukuma seed because it reflects integrity, honesty, and healing. In its original context, it is connected to traditional medicinal practices and represents protection as well as balance across the body, mind, and spirit.

Building on this foundation, Steward translates these principles into an Electronic Health Record (EHR) case management system designed around the realities of front-line mental healthcare work. The platform’s aim is to reduce cognitive load, protect sensitive information, create space for meaningful human interaction and more. At the same time, Steward reimagines social work documentation as a more engaging and rewarding experience, introducing moments of enjoyment within a process that is often associated with stress or monotony.

Steward is designed with the belief that when we focus on the needs of those who are dedicated to uplifting others, we strengthen society as a whole. The idea that “a rising tide lifts all boats” serves as my core tenet, where technology is used not just to optimize output, but to foster dignity and long-term well-being across people and their environments.
